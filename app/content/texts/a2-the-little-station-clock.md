---
title: "The Little Station Clock"
level: "a2"
date: "2026-06-24"
---

Every morning, Daniel walked past the old train station on his way to work. The station was small and quiet because most people now used the new bus road across town. Only one train stopped there each day, just after sunrise.

On the wall above the ticket window, there was a round clock with black numbers and two thin hands. Daniel liked the clock because it reminded him of his grandfather, who had worked at the station many years before.

One Tuesday, Daniel noticed that the clock had stopped. Its hands showed seven fifteen, but it was already eight o'clock. The station looked strange without the quiet movement of the clock.

Daniel went to work, but he kept thinking about it. During his lunch break, he bought a small battery from a shop. After work, he returned to the station and found the door open. The waiting room smelled of dust and old wood.

He stood on a chair and opened the back of the clock. The battery was old and white at the edges. Daniel changed it carefully, cleaned the glass with his sleeve, and put the clock back on the wall.

For a moment, nothing happened. Then the long hand moved. Tick. Tick. Tick. The sound filled the empty room.

The next morning, an old woman sat on the station bench with a suitcase beside her. She looked up at the clock and smiled. Daniel did not know her, but he felt happy. The station seemed awake again.

After that day, Daniel stopped for a minute each morning. He checked the clock, watched the first train arrive, and remembered his grandfather. The little station was still quiet, but it no longer felt forgotten.
