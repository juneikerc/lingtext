---
title: "Cooking My Favorite Meal"
level: "a2"
---

I love cooking for my friends and family. It is one of my favorite hobbies because it allows me to be creative and make people happy at the same time. My favorite dish to make is pasta with a special homemade tomato sauce. This is a recipe that my grandmother taught me when I was a child, and I have practiced it many times since then.

First, I need to make sure I have all the necessary ingredients. I go to the local market to buy fresh tomatoes, onions, garlic, and herbs. I believe that using fresh ingredients is the secret to a great meal. I also buy a high-quality packet of spaghetti and some parmesan cheese. Once I am back in my kitchen, I start the preparation process. I put on my favorite apron and turn on some light music to create a pleasant atmosphere.

The first step in the actual cooking is to boil water in a large pot. I fill it about three-quarters full and place it on the stove over high heat. I add a generous amount of salt to the water. Some people say the water should taste like the sea. While waiting for the water to boil, I begin preparing the vegetables. I carefully peel the onions and chop them into very small pieces. I do the same with the garlic, but I prefer to crush it slightly before chopping to release more of its flavor.

Once the onions and garlic are ready, I heat some extra virgin olive oil in a large frying pan. When the oil is hot, I add the onions. I cook them slowly until they become soft and translucent. This takes about five to seven minutes. Then, I add the garlic. I have to be careful not to burn the garlic because it can become bitter very quickly. After a minute, the kitchen begins to smell wonderful.

Next, I add the tomatoes. I use a combination of fresh chopped tomatoes and a little bit of tomato puree for a richer texture. I stir everything together and add some salt, black pepper, and a pinch of sugar. The sugar helps to balance the acidity of the tomatoes. I lower the heat and let the sauce simmer gently. This is the most important part of the process because the flavors need time to blend together.

By now, the water in the pot should be boiling rapidly. I add the pasta and stir it occasionally to make sure the strands do not stick together. I check the instructions on the packet for the cooking time, but I usually test it a minute before it is supposed to be done. I like my pasta "al dente," which means it should still have a little bit of firmness when you bite into it.

While the pasta and sauce are cooking, I prepare the final touches. I wash some fresh basil leaves and tear them into small pieces. I also grate a generous amount of parmesan cheese. I set the dining table with a nice tablecloth, candles, and glasses. I want my friends to feel special and welcome in my home.

When the pasta is ready, I drain it in a colander, but I always save a small cup of the pasta water. I add the pasta directly into the frying pan with the tomato sauce. I pour in a little bit of the reserved pasta water, which helps the sauce stick to the pasta perfectly. I toss everything together over low heat for one more minute. Finally, I add the fresh basil and a drizzle of olive oil.

When my friends arrive, they are greeted by the delicious aroma of the meal. We sit at the table and I serve the pasta onto large plates. Everyone says my pasta is the best, and seeing their smiles makes all the effort worth it. We talk, laugh, and enjoy the food together. For me, cooking is not just about eating; it is about sharing love and creating memories with the people who matter most.

After we finish eating, we often stay at the table for a long time, talking about our lives and future plans. We might have some fresh fruit or a small dessert, but the highlight is always the main course. Cleaning up the kitchen afterwards is also a part of the experience. My friends often help me wash the dishes while we continue our conversation.

In conclusion, cooking my favorite meal is a ritual that brings me great joy. It starts with selecting the best ingredients and ends with a shared experience around the table. It reminds me of my childhood and the lessons I learned from my grandmother. Every time I cook this pasta, I feel a connection to my family history and a sense of pride in my own skills. I hope to keep improving my cooking and sharing it with even more people in the future. Food has a unique way of bringing people together, and I am happy to play a small part in that.
