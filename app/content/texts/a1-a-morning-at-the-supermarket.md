---
title: "A Morning at the Supermarket"
level: "a1"
date: "2026-04-12"
---

Emma and her mother go to the supermarket. It is Saturday morning. The sun is bright and warm.

**Emma:** The store is big!

**Mother:** Yes. We need to buy food for the week.

**Emma:** What do we need?

**Mother:** We need milk, bread, eggs, and fruit.

They walk through the door. Emma takes a small cart. She likes to push it.

**Emma:** Can I help you?

**Mother:** Yes, please. Find some apples. They are over there.

Emma walks to the fruit area. She sees red apples and green apples. She picks six red apples. They look good.

**Emma:** Here are the apples, Mom.

**Mother:** Thank you. These are very nice.

They go to the next area. Emma sees many types of bread.

**Emma:** Which bread do you want?

**Mother:** The brown bread, please. It is healthy.

Emma puts one loaf of brown bread in the cart.

**Emma:** Do we need milk?

**Mother:** Yes. We need two bottles.

They walk to the cold area. Emma opens the door. She takes two bottles of milk. They are cold.

**Emma:** The milk is very cold!

**Mother:** Put it in the cart quickly.

Next, they look for eggs. Emma finds a box with twelve eggs.

**Emma:** Are these okay?

**Mother:** Yes. Check that none are broken.

Emma looks at each egg. They are all good.

**Emma:** No broken eggs!

**Mother:** Great. Now we need vegetables.

They walk to the vegetable area. There are tomatoes, carrots, and onions. Emma likes the red tomatoes.

**Emma:** Can we get some tomatoes?

**Mother:** Of course. Get four big ones.

Emma picks four tomatoes. She puts them in a small bag.

**Emma:** What else?

**Mother:** Some carrots, please.

Emma takes a bag of carrots. They are orange and long.

**Mother:** Do you want anything special, Emma?

**Emma:** Can we buy some cookies?

**Mother:** Okay. But only one box.

Emma smiles. She finds a box of chocolate cookies. She puts them in the cart.

**Emma:** Thank you, Mom!

**Mother:** You are a good helper today.

They go to the front of the store. There is a line of people. They wait for their turn.

**Emma:** The line is long.

**Mother:** Yes. But we are almost done.

A man in front of them pays for his food. Then it is their turn.

**Mother:** Hello. We are ready.

The woman at the counter smiles. She takes the milk, bread, eggs, apples, tomatoes, carrots, and cookies.

**Cashier:** That will be twenty-five dollars.

**Mother:** Here you go.

The cashier puts everything in bags. Emma helps carry a small bag.

**Emma:** I can carry this one.

**Mother:** Thank you, Emma. You are very strong.

They walk out of the store. The sun is still shining.

**Emma:** I like shopping with you.

**Mother:** I like it too. Maybe next week you can choose the fruit.

**Emma:** Really? I would love that!

**Mother:** Now let us go home and make lunch.

**Emma:** Can we have sandwiches?

**Mother:** Yes. With the new bread.

They walk to their car. Emma is happy. She helped her mother, and they have good food for the week.

**Emma:** This was a good morning.

**Mother:** Yes. The best mornings are when we are together.

Emma puts on her seat belt. She looks at the bags of food.

**Emma:** I want to learn to cook someday.

**Mother:** You will. Little by little.

They drive home together. Emma looks out the window. She sees people walking in the street. She sees a dog running in the park.

**Emma:** Today is a good day.

**Mother:** Every day is good when we have food and family.

Emma agrees. She cannot wait to eat lunch with her mother.
