# A Visit to the Aquarium

Last month, Sofia and her cousin Leo went to the city aquarium. They had wanted to go for a long time. Sofia was fifteen years old, and Leo was twelve. Their aunt bought the tickets online and drove them there on a cloudy Saturday morning.

The aquarium was next to the river. The building looked like a giant glass wave. Many families and school groups were already waiting at the entrance.

**Leo:** Wow! This place is huge!

**Sofia:** Yes. I read that it has over five hundred different species.

**Leo:** I want to see the sharks first.

**Sofia:** Okay. But let us look at the map. We do not want to get lost.

They entered the main hall. The first room was full of tropical fish. The walls were covered with enormous tanks. Everywhere they looked, they saw bright colors: yellow fish, blue fish, orange fish with white stripes.

**Leo:** Look at that one! It looks like a clown!

**Sofia:** That is a clownfish. They live near coral reefs.

A small turtle swam past the glass. It moved slowly but gracefully.

**Leo:** The turtle is so calm.

**Sofia:** Sea turtles can live for more than eighty years.

They walked into a dark tunnel. The tunnel had glass walls, and water was all around them. Big fish swam overhead. Suddenly, a giant stingray glided above their heads. Its body was flat and wide, like a flying carpet.

**Leo:** Did you see that?

**Sofia:** Yes! It was beautiful.

Next, they found the jellyfish room. It was very quiet and dark, with soft blue lights. Hundreds of jellyfish floated up and down like living lamps. Some were small and white. Others were pink and almost transparent.

**Leo:** They look like they are from another planet.

**Sofia:** They do not have brains or hearts. But they have been on Earth for millions of years.

**Leo:** That is amazing.

They took many photos. Then they moved to the shark tank. It was enormous. Grey sharks swam in slow circles. Their eyes looked serious and ancient.

**Leo:** They are scary.

**Sofia:** They are not really dangerous to people. Most sharks are very shy.

A guide was standing nearby. She started talking about shark conservation.

**Guide:** Sharks are very important for the ocean. Without them, other fish populations grow too large.

**Leo:** I did not know that.

**Sofia:** We need to protect them.

After the sharks, they visited the penguin area. The penguins lived in a cold room with ice and rocks. Some stood still like little guards. Others jumped into the water and swam very fast.

**Leo:** They look so funny when they walk!

**Sofia:** And they swim better than they walk.

A keeper came to feed them. The penguins made loud calls and waddled toward the food.

**Leo:** Do they always eat fish?

**Keeper:** Yes, fish is their main food. They also eat small shrimp.

By noon, Sofia and Leo were hungry. They went to the aquarium cafe. While they ate their sandwiches, they watched the river through the window.

**Sofia:** This was a great idea.

**Leo:** I want to be a marine biologist someday.

**Sofia:** You would be very good at it. You ask so many questions.

In the afternoon, they watched a show at the main tank. A diver went inside and fed the biggest fish by hand. The children in the audience clapped and cheered.

**Leo:** Can I do that too?

**Sofia:** Maybe when you are older.

They visited the gift shop before leaving. Leo bought a small stuffed shark. Sofia chose a book about ocean animals.

**Leo:** Thank you for bringing me here.

**Sofia:** I had a wonderful time too. Let us come back next year.

They walked to the car as the rain started to fall. It did not matter. They were talking and laughing about all the things they had seen.
