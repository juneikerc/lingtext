---
title: "The Best Gift I Ever Received"
level: "b1"
date: "2026-05-08"
---

People often remember special days because of the gifts they receive. Some gifts are expensive, while others are small and simple. In my opinion, the best gift I have ever received was not something I could buy in a shop. It was a small notebook that my grandfather gave me on my twelfth birthday.

At that time, my grandfather was already very old. He walked slowly and could not see well, but his mind was still sharp. Every time I visited him, he told me stories about his travels and the people he had met. I loved listening to him because his stories were full of adventure and humor.

On my birthday, he called me to his room and gave me a small leather notebook. It was old and the cover was a little damaged, but I could see that he had kept it carefully for many years. "This was my travel journal," he said with a smile. "I wrote in it every day when I was young and full of energy. Now I want you to have it because I think you will understand its value someday."

I opened the notebook and saw his handwriting. The pages were full of descriptions of mountains, rivers, and cities he had visited many years ago. He wrote about the food he tried, the friends he made, and the mistakes he learned from. Some pages had small drawings of birds and flowers. Others had tickets and stamps from different countries.

At first, I did not understand why he gave it to me. I was only twelve, and I had never traveled far from my hometown. However, my grandfather explained that the notebook was not just about travel. It was about paying attention to life and remembering small moments. He told me that every day has something interesting if you look carefully. "Write down what you see," he said. "One day, you will be happy that you did. Memories fade, but words stay."

Because of his advice, I started writing in my own notebook every evening. At first, I only wrote simple things like what I ate for dinner or what the weather was like. After a few months, I began to add my feelings, my dreams, and my questions about the future. When I was sad, writing helped me understand why. When I was happy, I wanted to remember every detail so I could read it again later.

Last year, my grandfather passed away. It was a difficult time for my family, and I missed him very much. During those sad weeks, I read his old notebook again. His words made me feel that he was still with me, telling me to be strong and curious. I realized that his gift was not just paper and ink. It was a way of seeing the world.

Now I have three notebooks full of my own stories. I write about my friends, my school, and the places I visit on weekends. Sometimes I draw small pictures like my grandfather did, even though I am not very good at art. Although my handwriting is not as beautiful as his, I know that he would be proud of me. I believe that keeping a journal is one of the best habits anyone can develop.

If someone asks me what the best gift is, I always say that it is something that helps you grow. Money cannot buy the feeling of opening an old notebook and remembering someone you love. I hope that one day I can give a similar gift to my own grandchildren.
