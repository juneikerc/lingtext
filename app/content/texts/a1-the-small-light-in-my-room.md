# The Small Light in My Room

My name is Nina. I am eleven years old. I live with my mother, my father, and my brother Tom. Our house is small, but it is warm and happy. My room is small too. I have a bed, a desk, a chair, a lamp, and a shelf with books.

Every day I wake up at seven o'clock. I hear birds outside and my mother in the kitchen. My brother is loud in the morning, but I am quiet.

One Monday night, I do my homework and then I go to my room to read. My lamp is on, and my room is quiet. I open my book about animals.

Then I see a small light under my desk.

I look closer. The light is coming from a small white box. The box is warm, and it has a little silver star on top. I touch it, and I hear a tiny voice.

“Hello,” the voice says.

I look around, but I see no one. “Hello,” I say. Then the box opens, and I see a very small boy with wings. “My name is Pip,” he says. “I am a light keeper.”

Pip tells me that he lost a heart light. He says every room has one, and it helps the room feel warm and safe. We look under the bed, behind the chair, and near the shelf, but we do not find it.

Then I look behind a photo frame on my shelf. There is a soft gold light there. It is very small, but it is warm and kind. Pip smiles. “You found it,” he says.

I put the light into the box. At once, the room feels softer and warmer. Pip says the room is well again. Then my father calls from the hall, so Pip closes the box and leaves.

In the morning, my room feels calm and nice. I do not see the light, but I feel it. Now I clean my room, open the window, and help my family more. I think small things are important.

Every night, I say, “Good night” to my room. And sometimes I think the room answers back.
