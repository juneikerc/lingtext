title: "Fixing Our Class Website"
level: "b1"
date: "2026-05-18"
---

Last month, our teacher asked us to improve the class website. It was an old page with homework dates, photos from school trips, and a few links to study materials. The problem was that nobody used it anymore. Some information was wrong, several links did not work, and the design looked confusing on a phone. Although I am not an expert, I like technology, so I offered to help.

First, we made a list of the main problems. Many students said they could not find the homework page quickly. Others wanted a calendar because they often forgot tests and project dates. Our teacher also wanted a place where she could post short messages without sending a long email every time. We decided that the website did not need to be beautiful. It needed to be simple, clear, and useful.

I worked with two classmates, Nora and Sam. Nora was good at writing, so she checked the old text and made it shorter. Sam liked taking photos, so he chose a few pictures that showed our school but did not include students' faces. I changed the menu and put the most important pages at the top. When something did not work, we asked our computer teacher for advice.

The hardest part was the calendar. We wanted everyone to see the same dates, but we also wanted the teacher to update them easily. At first, we tried a complicated tool, but it was slow and asked for too many passwords. In the end, we used a simple shared calendar. It was not perfect, but it solved the real problem. Sometimes the best answer is the one that people can actually use.

After two weeks, we showed the new website to the class. Some students noticed small mistakes, and one student said the colors were too dark. We fixed these details the next day. Then our teacher posted the link in the class chat. By the end of the week, most students had visited the site at least once.

I learned that technology is not only about computers. It is also about listening to people and understanding what they need. If we had changed the website without asking questions, we would probably have built something useless. Now the site helps students remember homework and tests. It is a small project, but I feel proud because it makes daily school life a little easier.
