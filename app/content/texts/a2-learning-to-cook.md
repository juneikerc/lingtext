# Learning to Cook

David decided to learn how to cook. He was tired of eating sandwiches and ordering pizza. He wanted to make healthy, delicious meals for himself. His friend Maria was an excellent cook, so he asked her to teach him.

**David:** Maria, I need your help. I want to learn how to cook.

**Maria:** That is a great idea! Cooking is fun and it saves money too. When do you want to start?

**David:** How about this weekend? I am free on Saturday afternoon.

**Maria:** Perfect. Come to my apartment at two o'clock. We can make pasta together.

**David:** Pasta sounds good. Is it difficult to make?

**Maria:** Not at all! It is one of the easiest dishes. But it tastes amazing when you do it right.

On Saturday, David arrived at Maria's apartment with a notebook. He wanted to write down everything she taught him.

**Maria:** You do not need a notebook, David. Cooking is about practice, not memorizing recipes.

**David:** But I want to remember the steps. What if I forget something?

**Maria:** Then you will make mistakes. That is how you learn. Do not worry, I will guide you.

They went to the kitchen. Maria had already bought all the ingredients.

**Maria:** First, we need to prepare the vegetables. Can you chop these tomatoes?

**David:** I have never chopped vegetables before. How do I do it?

**Maria:** Hold the knife like this. Keep your fingers away from the blade. Cut slowly at first.

David tried to chop the tomatoes. Some pieces were big, some were small. It was not perfect, but he was trying.

**Maria:** Good start! You will get better with practice. Now, let us chop the onions.

**David:** Onions make me cry. Is there a way to stop that?

**Maria:** Yes! Put the onions in the refrigerator for thirty minutes before you cut them. The cold temperature helps.

**David:** I will remember that tip. What is next?

**Maria:** Now we heat some olive oil in this pan. When it is hot, we add the onions and garlic.

The kitchen started to smell wonderful. David felt excited.

**David:** This smells delicious already!

**Maria:** Wait until you taste the final result. Now, stir the onions gently. Do not let them burn.

David stirred carefully. The onions became soft and golden.

**Maria:** Excellent! Now add the tomatoes. Let them cook for about fifteen minutes.

**David:** What about the pasta? When do we cook that?

**Maria:** We cook the pasta in a separate pot. We need to boil water first. Put a large pot on the stove.

David filled the pot with water and put it on the stove.

**Maria:** Always add salt to the water. It should taste like the sea. This gives flavor to the pasta.

**David:** How much salt do I put?

**Maria:** About one tablespoon for this amount of water. You will learn to judge by taste.

While they waited for the water to boil, Maria showed David how to make a simple salad.

**Maria:** A good meal needs vegetables. This salad is easy. Just mix lettuce, cucumber, and olive oil.

**David:** That looks simple enough. I think I can make this alone.

**Maria:** I am sure you can. The key is using fresh ingredients.

The water started to boil. Big bubbles rose to the surface.

**Maria:** Now we add the pasta. Put it in slowly so the hot water does not splash.

David added the pasta carefully. It sank into the water.

**Maria:** Stir it once so it does not stick together. Then we wait about ten minutes.

**David:** How do I know when it is ready?

**Maria:** Take one piece out and taste it. It should be soft but still a little firm. We call that "al dente" in Italian.

**David:** Al dente. I like that expression.

While the pasta cooked, they checked the tomato sauce. It was bubbling gently and smelled amazing.

**Maria:** The sauce is ready. Let us taste it. Here, try a spoon.

David tasted the sauce. It was warm and flavorful.

**David:** This is fantastic! Did you add herbs?

**Maria:** Yes, I added basil and a little oregano. You can add any herbs you like.

**David:** I can taste the garlic too. Everything works together perfectly.

**Maria:** That is the magic of cooking. Simple ingredients become something special.

The pasta was ready. David drained it in the sink.

**Maria:** Be careful! The steam is very hot. Use oven mitts to hold the pot.

David carefully poured the pasta into a colander. Steam rose up around him.

**Maria:** Now put the pasta back in the pot and add the sauce. Mix everything together.

David mixed the pasta and sauce. The red sauce coated every piece.

**Maria:** Beautiful! Now let us serve it. Put some pasta on each plate. Add the salad on the side.

They sat at the table with their meal. David took his first bite.

**David:** Maria, this is incredible! I cannot believe I helped make this.

**Maria:** You did more than help. You made most of it yourself. How do you feel?

**David:** Proud! And full. This is much better than pizza.

**Maria:** I am glad you like it. Cooking is a skill that takes time, but every meal you make teaches you something new.

**David:** Can you teach me more recipes? I want to learn everything.

**Maria:** Of course! Next week we can make chicken with vegetables. It is healthy and easy.

**David:** I will buy the ingredients this time. It is my turn to prepare.

**Maria:** That sounds perfect. Cooking together is more fun than cooking alone.

**David:** Thank you for teaching me, Maria. This was a wonderful afternoon.

**Maria:** You are welcome, David. Now you can cook for yourself. And maybe someday, you will cook for someone special.

**David:** I hope so! But first, I need to practice more.

They finished their meal and talked about different foods from around the world. Maria told David about the dishes her grandmother used to make. David shared stories about his mother's cooking.

**David:** My mother makes the best soup. Maybe I can learn that recipe too.

**Maria:** Absolutely! Cooking family recipes keeps traditions alive.

**David:** I never thought cooking could be so meaningful. It is not just about food.

**Maria:** You are right. It is about love, culture, and sharing. Every dish tells a story.

**David:** I want to learn those stories. This is just the beginning.

Maria smiled. She was happy to share her passion with her friend.

**Maria:** Welcome to the world of cooking, David. Your journey starts now.

David left Maria's apartment with a full stomach and a happy heart. He could not wait to cook his next meal.
