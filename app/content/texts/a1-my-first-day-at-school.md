# My First Day at School

**Anna:** Good morning! My name is Anna. What is your name?

**Tom:** Hello! I am Tom. Nice to meet you.

**Anna:** Nice to meet you too! Is this your first day here?

**Tom:** Yes, it is. I am new. I do not know anyone.

**Anna:** Do not worry. I can help you. I come here every day.

**Tom:** Thank you! That is very kind.

**Anna:** How old are you, Tom?

**Tom:** I am seven years old. And you?

**Anna:** I am seven too! We are the same age.

**Tom:** Do you like this school?

**Anna:** Yes, I like it very much. The teachers are nice. The food is good too.

**Tom:** I am hungry now. Is it time for lunch?

**Anna:** No, not yet. We have one more class. Then we eat.

**Tom:** Okay. What do we learn today?

**Anna:** We learn about animals. Do you like animals?

**Tom:** Yes! I like dogs. I have a dog at home. His name is Max.

**Anna:** That is a good name! I like cats. I have two cats.

**Tom:** What are their names?

**Anna:** One is Luna. One is Milo.

**Tom:** Those are nice names. What color are they?

**Anna:** Luna is white. Milo is black and orange.

**Tom:** I want to see them someday.

**Anna:** Maybe you can come to my house. We can play with the cats.

**Tom:** That sounds fun! I will ask my mother.

**Anna:** Good. Now, let us go to class. The teacher is waiting.

**Tom:** Okay. I am a little nervous.

**Anna:** Do not be nervous. The teacher is very kind. Her name is Mrs. Brown.

**Tom:** Mrs. Brown. I will remember.

They walk to the classroom together. The room is big and bright. There are many desks and chairs. There are pictures on the walls.

**Mrs. Brown:** Good morning, class!

**All Students:** Good morning, Mrs. Brown!

**Mrs. Brown:** Today we have a new student. Tom, please stand up.

Tom stands up. His face is red. He is shy.

**Tom:** Hello. I am Tom. I am seven years old. I am happy to be here.

**Mrs. Brown:** Thank you, Tom. Please sit next to Anna.

Tom sits down. Anna smiles at him.

**Anna:** See? It is not so bad.

**Tom:** You are right. Thank you for helping me.

**Mrs. Brown:** Today we learn about farm animals. Who knows what a farm is?

**Anna:** I know! A farm is a place with animals. Cows live there. Pigs live there too.

**Mrs. Brown:** Very good, Anna! Tom, do you know any farm animals?

**Tom:** Yes. Chickens live on farms. They give us eggs.

**Mrs. Brown:** Excellent! Chickens are very important on farms.

The class continues. The students draw pictures of animals. Tom draws a dog. Anna draws her cats.

**Tom:** Look at my picture. This is Max.

**Anna:** It is very good! Max looks happy.

**Tom:** Thank you. Your cats look beautiful.

**Anna:** I love drawing. Do you like to draw?

**Tom:** Yes, but I am not very good.

**Anna:** You are good! You just need practice. I can help you.

**Tom:** Really? That would be wonderful.

**Anna:** We can draw together every day at lunch time.

**Tom:** I would like that very much.

The bell rings. It is time for lunch.

**Mrs. Brown:** Class is over for now. Go eat your lunch.

**All Students:** Thank you, Mrs. Brown!

Anna and Tom walk to the lunch room together.

**Anna:** What food did you bring?

**Tom:** My mother made me a sandwich. It has cheese and tomato. I also have an apple.

**Anna:** I have soup and bread. And a banana for dessert.

**Tom:** Do you want to sit together?

**Anna:** Yes! Let us find a table.

They sit at a table near the window. The sun is shining outside.

**Tom:** This school is nice. The children are friendly.

**Anna:** Yes, everyone is kind here. You will make many friends.

**Tom:** I already have one friend. You!

**Anna:** Thank you, Tom. You are my friend too.

They eat their lunch and talk about their favorite things. Tom likes to play soccer. Anna likes to read books.

**Tom:** Do you play soccer?

**Anna:** No, I do not. But I can learn. Will you teach me?

**Tom:** Yes! We can play after school.

**Anna:** Great! My mother can pick us up.

**Tom:** I need to ask my mother first.

**Anna:** Okay. Ask her today.

After lunch, they have math class. The teacher gives them numbers to add.

**Tom:** This is hard. I do not understand.

**Anna:** I can help. Look, we put this number here. Then we add this one.

**Tom:** Oh! Now I see. Thank you, Anna.

**Anna:** You are welcome. Math is easy when you practice.

The day goes by quickly. Soon it is time to go home.

**Mrs. Brown:** Goodbye, class! See you tomorrow.

**All Students:** Goodbye, Mrs. Brown!

Anna and Tom walk to the school door together.

**Tom:** Today was a good day. I am not nervous anymore.

**Anna:** I am happy to hear that. School is fun when you have friends.

**Tom:** Will you be here tomorrow?

**Anna:** Yes, I come every day. I will see you tomorrow.

**Tom:** Good! I will bring a picture of Max to show you.

**Anna:** And I will bring photos of Luna and Milo.

**Tom:** Perfect! See you tomorrow, Anna.

**Anna:** See you tomorrow, Tom. Have a good evening!

**Tom:** You too! Thank you for everything.

Tom walks to his mother. He is smiling. Anna waves goodbye. She is happy to have a new friend.

Tomorrow will be another good day at school.
