title: "A Checkup at the Health Center"
level: "a2"
date: "2026-05-18"
---

Yesterday morning, I went to the health center for a checkup. I was not sick, but my school asked all students to visit a nurse once a year. My appointment was at nine o'clock, so I left home at half past eight. The health center is next to the bus station, and it only takes fifteen minutes to get there.

When I arrived, I gave my name to the woman at the front desk. She asked me to sit in the waiting room. There were six people there. An old man read a newspaper, and a young mother talked quietly to her baby. The room was clean and bright, and there were pictures about healthy food on the wall.

After ten minutes, the nurse called my name. She was friendly and asked me simple questions. Did I sleep well? Did I eat breakfast? Did I do exercise? I told her that I usually sleep well, but I do not always eat breakfast because I am often late for school.

Then she checked my height, my weight, and my eyes. She also listened to my heart. Nothing hurt, and the checkup was quick. The nurse said I was healthy, but she gave me some advice. She told me to drink more water and to eat something small in the morning, even when I am busy.

Before I left, she gave me a paper with ideas for easy breakfasts. There were bananas, eggs, bread, and yogurt on the list. I liked the ideas because they were not expensive. On the way home, I bought yogurt and fruit at a small store.

Today, I ate breakfast before school. It only took five minutes, but I felt better in class. I think the nurse was right. Small habits can help your body, and a checkup can teach you something useful.
