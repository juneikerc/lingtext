title: "A simple talk in the park (chat)"
level: "a1"
---

Anna and Mark are friends. It is morning. They are in a park **today**. The sun is warm. The sky is blue. Anna has a small bag. Mark has a **glass** bottle of water in his hand. They sit on a bench and talk **about** their day, their families, and their **life**.

**Anna:** Hello, Mark! How are you **today**?

**Mark:** Hello, Anna! I am good, thank you. And you?

**Anna:** I am happy **today**. It is a very nice day.

**Mark:** Yes, it is a beautiful day. I like the sun and the fresh air.

**Anna:** Me too. What do you have in your hand?

**Mark:** I have a **glass** bottle of water. I always **take** water with me.

**Anna:** That is a good idea. I have juice in my bag.

**Mark:** Nice! Do you have your phone too?

**Anna:** Yes, I do. My phone is in my **pocket** now.

**Mark:** Your **pocket** is big then!

**Anna:** Yes, it is a big coat. It has a big **pocket**.

**Mark:** That is funny. Can you **tell** me **about** your morning?

**Anna:** Yes. I wake up at seven. Then I **take** a shower. After that, I **take** my bag and I go out. I buy bread and milk. Then I come to the park.

**Mark:** That sounds like a good morning.

**Anna:** And you? Can you **tell** me **about** your morning?

**Mark:** Sure. I wake up at six-thirty. I **take** my dog to the street. Then I eat breakfast. I **take** tea and toast. After that, I come here.

**Anna:** Very nice. I like tea too.

**Mark:** Anna, can I **tell** you something funny?

**Anna:** Yes, please **tell** me!

**Mark:** This morning, I look for my keys in my jacket **pocket**, but the keys are not there.

**Anna:** Oh no! Where are they?

**Mark:** They are in my other **pocket**!

**Anna:** That is funny! Sometimes I do that too.

**Mark:** I **think** many people do that.

**Anna:** Yes, I **think** so too.

**Mark:** What do you **think** **about** this park?

**Anna:** I **think** it is quiet and beautiful. I like to sit here and read.

**Mark:** I **think** the same. I come here when I want to **think** **about** my **life**.

**Anna:** That is interesting. What do you **think** **about** in your **life**?

**Mark:** I **think** **about** my family, my work, and my future. Sometimes I **think** **about** small things too, like food or music.

**Anna:** Yes, me too. I **think** **about** my mother and father. I **think** **about** my little sister too.

**Mark:** Family is important in our **whole** **life**.

**Anna:** Yes, family is a big part of our **whole** **life**.

**Mark:** Can you **tell** me **about** your sister?

**Anna:** Yes. Her name is Lucy. She is ten years old. She likes school and animals. She can **tell** very funny stories.

**Mark:** That is lovely. My brother can **tell** funny stories too.

**Anna:** Do you live with your brother?

**Mark:** No, I do not. He lives in another town now. But we talk every week **about** work, friends, and **life**.

**Anna:** That is very nice. I like to talk **about** **life** with friends. It helps me.

**Mark:** Yes, me too. A good friend can **tell** you kind words.

**Anna:** Mark, what do you want to do **today** after the park?

**Mark:** I want to **take** the bus to the city center. I want to buy a book.

**Anna:** Oh, very nice. What book do you want to **take** home?

**Mark:** I want a simple English book. I want to read and learn new words.

**Anna:** That is great. I **think** reading is very good for English.

**Mark:** Yes, I **think** so. And you, what do you want to do **today**?

**Anna:** I want to go home and cook lunch. Then I want to call my mother and **tell** her **about** my day.

**Mark:** What do you want to cook?

**Anna:** Rice, chicken, and vegetables. It is not a big meal, but it is good.

**Mark:** That sounds good. I will **take** some fruit home and make a small salad.

**Anna:** Healthy food is good for the **whole** body.

**Mark:** Yes, and a good day is good for the **whole** **life**.

**Anna:** That is true. Sometimes small happy moments change the **whole** day.

**Mark:** Yes. A walk, a smile, a talk with a friend, a little sun, a **glass** of water — all these things are simple, but important.

**Anna:** I like that. A **glass** of water, a friend, and a quiet park can make **today** special.

**Mark:** Yes. **Today** is special because we can talk and relax.

**Anna:** I **think** this is a very good **today**.

**Mark:** Me too. Thank you for this talk.

**Anna:** Thank you too. You always **tell** good stories.

**Mark:** And you always make me **think** **about** good things in **life**.

**Anna:** That is sweet. Now, let us stand up.

**Mark:** Yes, let us go. I will **take** my bottle.

**Anna:** And I will **take** my bag.

**Mark:** Is your phone still in your **pocket**?

**Anna:** Yes, it is! This time I know where it is.

**Mark:** Very good! Let us enjoy **today**.

**Anna:** Yes, let us enjoy the **whole** day.

**Mark:** And let us **think** **about** happy things.

**Anna:** Yes, because **life** is better with happy thoughts.

**Mark:** I **think** that is true.

**Anna:** Me too.
