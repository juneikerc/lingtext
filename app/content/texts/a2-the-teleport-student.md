# Chapter 1 — The Letter from the Mountain

Leo Parker was sixteen years old. He lived in a small town near a river with his mother. Their house was not big, but it was warm and comfortable. Leo liked simple things. He liked reading adventure books, walking near the river, and watching the stars at night. His dream was to travel and see the world, but his family did not have much money.

Leo’s father disappeared many years ago. His mother never talked much about him. She only said, "Your father was a brave man."

One rainy afternoon, Leo came home from school. He put his bag on the floor and walked into the kitchen.

"Mom, I’m home," he said.

Then he saw a strange letter on the table.

The paper was white and thick. There was a silver moon on it.

"Who sent this?" Leo asked.

His mother looked at him and smiled softly.

"Open it."

Leo carefully opened the letter.

Inside, he read:

_"Dear Leo Parker,
You have been accepted to Star Moon Academy.
Please arrive in three days."_

Leo read it again.

"Academy? But… I never applied."

His mother sat down.

"There are things about your father you do not know."

Leo looked at her in surprise.

"Dad?"

She nodded.

"He studied there. And now… it is your turn."

Leo could not sleep that night. He had many questions.

Was magic real?

Why did his father never return?

And why did this academy choose him?

Three days later, Leo packed his clothes, books, and a small picture of his family.

When the train arrived near the mountain, Leo looked outside the window.

There, above the clouds, he saw a giant castle.

His heart started beating faster.

His new life was about to begin.

# Chapter 2 — The Weak Power

The next morning, Leo walked through the academy gates. Everything looked incredible. There were tall towers, shining windows, and gardens full of strange flowers. Some students were flying on magic boards. Others were practicing spells in the open fields.

Leo felt nervous.

"Everyone here looks amazing," he thought.

A teacher named Professor Arden guided the new students into a large classroom.

"Today," the professor said, "we will test your magic power."

One by one, students touched a large blue crystal.

A girl created fire in her hands.

A boy made ice appear in the air.

Another student controlled the wind.

The class clapped each time.

Then it was Leo’s turn.

He slowly walked to the crystal.

"Touch it," said Professor Arden.

Leo put his hand on the crystal.

Suddenly—

_Flash!_

Leo disappeared.

The class shouted.

Then Leo appeared a few meters away.

He almost fell.

"What happened?" he asked.

Professor Arden looked surprised.

"Interesting…"

A student laughed.

"He only moved three meters!"

Another student smiled.

"That power is useless."

Leo felt his face become red.

He looked down.

Professor Arden raised his hand.

"Silence."

The room became quiet.

Then the professor looked at Leo.

"Teleportation magic is very rare."

The students stopped laughing.

"But…" the professor continued, "rare does not always mean easy."

Leo listened carefully.

"Your power may look small now," said the professor, "but if you train your body, your mind, and your magic, it may become something special."

That night, Leo sat alone in his room.

He looked at his hands.

"Maybe I’m weak now," he whispered.

"But I will become stronger."

For the first time, he believed he had a chance.

# Chapter 3 — Friends and Training

During his first week at the academy, Leo still felt different from the other students. Many students ignored him. Some laughed when they saw his short teleportation.

But not everyone.

One afternoon, Leo was practicing alone near the training field.

_Flash._

He moved three meters.

_Flash._

Then again.

After many tries, he fell to the ground.

"You train hard."

Leo looked up.

A girl with blue eyes smiled at him.

"My name is Emma," she said. "I use water magic."

Next to her stood a tall boy with black hair.

"I’m Noah," he said. "Sword class."

Leo stood up.

"I’m Leo."

Emma smiled.

"We know."

Leo laughed.

"Because of the embarrassing test?"

Emma nodded.

"A little."

The three students started talking.

Soon, they began training together every day.

Emma practiced making water shields.

Noah practiced fast sword attacks.

Leo practiced teleportation.

Sometimes he failed.

Sometimes he fell.

Sometimes he felt like giving up.

But his friends never laughed.

"Again," Noah always said.

"You can do it," Emma said.

After two weeks, Leo improved.

He could teleport five meters.

Then seven.

Then ten.

One evening, after training, the three friends sat under a tree.

Emma looked at Leo.

"You changed."

Leo smiled.

"Because I’m stronger?"

Emma shook her head.

"No. Because now… you believe in yourself."

Leo looked at the sunset.

Maybe this academy was not only about magic.

Maybe it was also about finding who he really was.

# Chapter 4 — The Dark Forest

A month later, the students received their first real mission.

Professor Arden stood in front of the class.

"Tomorrow, you will enter the Dark Forest."

Some students looked excited.

Others looked afraid.

"Your mission is simple," said the professor. "Find the blue crystal and return safely."

The next morning, Leo, Emma, and Noah entered the forest together.

The trees were tall and dark. Strange sounds came from everywhere.

Emma held her staff tightly.

"I don’t like this place."

Noah smiled.

"That means you’re paying attention."

After walking for an hour, they found a cave.

Inside, a blue light shined.

"The crystal," Leo said.

But suddenly—

_ROAR!_

A giant black wolf appeared.

Its red eyes looked at them.

Emma stepped back.

Noah pulled out his sword.

The wolf jumped toward Emma.

Without thinking—

_Flash!_

Leo teleported in front of her.

He pushed her away.

The wolf missed.

Noah attacked.

Emma used water chains.

Leo teleported around the wolf, confusing it.

Together, they fought.

Finally, Noah landed the last attack.

The wolf fell.

The forest became quiet again.

Emma looked at Leo.

"You saved me."

Leo smiled.

"I didn’t think. I just moved."

Noah put his sword away.

"That is what heroes do."

Leo looked at his hands.

For the first time, he understood.

His power was not weak.

It was made to protect people.

# Chapter 5 — The Secret Underground

One night, Leo could not sleep.

He walked through the quiet academy halls.

Then he saw something strange.

A small blue light moved across the floor.

Leo followed it.

The light stopped near an old wall.

When Leo touched the wall—

_Click._

A secret door opened.

Leo’s eyes became wide.

He walked down stone stairs.

At the bottom, he found an old room.

There were books, pictures, and old weapons.

Then Leo saw something that made him stop.

A picture of his father.

Next to him stood Professor Arden.

Leo picked up the picture.

"What is this?"

Then he found an old book.

He opened the first page.

It said:

_"The teleport mage will return when darkness rises again."_

Leo’s hands started shaking.

Suddenly—

"Interesting, isn’t it?"

Leo turned around.

Professor Arden stood behind him.

"You knew?" Leo asked.

The professor nodded.

"Yes."

"Tell me the truth."

Professor Arden looked serious.

"Your father was one of the strongest mages in history."

Leo could not believe it.

"And now," said the professor, "his enemy is coming back."

Leo felt cold.

"Why are you telling me this now?"

Professor Arden smiled.

"Because your training is over."

Leo looked confused.

"Now your real story begins."

# Chapter 6 — The Hero’s Choice

The next day, the academy was not peaceful.

Dark clouds covered the sky.

Alarm bells rang everywhere.

Students ran across the school.

Leo, Emma, and Noah stood with Professor Arden.

"He’s here," said the professor.

"Who?" Leo asked.

"The Shadow King."

A dark figure appeared in the sky.

His magic filled the air with fear.

Many students could not move.

Leo’s hands shook.

"I’m scared."

Emma held his arm.

"So are we."

Noah smiled.

"But we fight together."

Leo took a deep breath.

He remembered his training.

His friends.

His father.

Then he stepped forward.

"Professor…"

Arden looked at him.

"Am I ready?"

The professor smiled.

"You were ready the day you chose to protect others."

Leo closed his eyes.

His magic started growing.

Blue light covered his body.

Then—

_FLASH!_

Leo disappeared.

This time, he did not move ten meters.

He moved across the battlefield.

Everyone looked in surprise.

Leo opened his eyes.

His power had grown.

The Shadow King smiled.

"So… the next teleport mage has appeared."

Leo stood tall.

He was no longer just a student.

He was ready to become a hero.

**The battle had only begun.**
