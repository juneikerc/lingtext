On Monday morning, Sofia woke **up** early because she wanted to start her day in a different way. Usually, she stayed in bed for ten more minutes, but that day she decided to get up quickly, open the window, and look outside. The street was quiet, and she could **see** the first people going to work. The sky was blue, and the air was fresh. Sofia smiled and thought, “Today I **can** do things better.”

She went to the kitchen and made breakfast. She had bread, fruit, and tea. Her brother Leo came into the room and asked, “Why are you so happy today?” Sofia laughed and **say**d, “Because I want to **change** **some** things in my life. I want to be more active, more positive, and more organized.”

After breakfast, Sofia wrote a small plan in her notebook. First, she wanted to walk to school instead of taking the bus. Second, she wanted to talk more in English class. Third, she wanted to help **some**one at home in the evening. Her mother looked at the notebook and asked **how** she was going to do all that in one day. Sofia answered, “I don’t know exactly, but I **can** try.”

On the way to school, Sofia met her friend Emma. Emma wanted to **leave** home later, but she was happy to walk with Sofia. They talked about music, homework, and their weekend plans. When they arrived at school, they could **see** **everyone** in the playground. **Everyone** was talking loudly because there was a school event that afternoon.

In English class, the teacher asked the students to speak about their daily routines. Sofia was nervous at first, but then she put her hand **up** and started to talk. She explained what she did every morning and what she wanted to **change**. The teacher smiled and said, “Very good, Sofia.” Her classmates listened, and **some** of them asked questions.

In the afternoon, the school event began. There were games, music, and food. Sofia helped put chairs in the hall and showed visitors **how** to find the gym. She felt useful and calm. At the end of the day, she was tired, but she was also proud.

When she got home, her mother asked, “So, **how** was your day?” Sofia answered, “It was great. I learned that small actions **can** make a big difference. You do not need to **change** **everyone** or everything. You only need to start with **some** simple steps.”

Before going to bed, Sofia wrote one last sentence in her notebook: “Tomorrow I will wake **up**, smile, and try again.”
