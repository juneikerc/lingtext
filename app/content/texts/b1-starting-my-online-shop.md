title: "Starting My Online Shop"
level: "b1"
date: "2026-04-22"
---

Three months ago, I started an online shop selling handmade jewelry. It was a dream I had for years, but I was always too afraid to try. Now, looking back, I am glad I finally took the risk.

I used to work in a big office from nine to five. The job was stable, and the money was good. However, every day felt the same. I sat at a computer, answered emails, and attended long meetings. I did not hate my job, but I did not love it either.

After work, I made jewelry as a hobby. I learned from online videos and practiced every evening. My friends loved my work and often asked me to make pieces for them. "You should sell these," they said. I always laughed and said it was just for fun.

Then, one day, everything changed. The company I worked for announced layoffs. My department would lose ten people. Although I was not fired, the news made me think about my future. Did I want to spend my whole life in an office, waiting for the next crisis?

I decided to start small. I opened an online shop on the weekend. It took me three days to take photos of my jewelry, write descriptions, and set up the website. I was nervous. What if nobody bought anything? What if people thought my work was bad?

The first month was difficult. Only two people visited my shop, and nobody bought anything. I felt discouraged. Maybe my friends were just being nice. Maybe my jewelry was not good enough to sell.

However, I did not give up. I read articles about online business and watched videos about marketing. I learned that I needed better photos and clearer descriptions. I also started using social media to show my work. Every day, I posted pictures of new pieces and told stories about how I made them.

Slowly, things began to change. More people visited my shop. After six weeks, I got my first sale. A woman from another city bought a pair of earrings for her daughter's birthday. When I saw the notification, I could not believe it. Someone I had never met wanted to buy something I made!

That first sale gave me hope. I worked harder than ever. I made new designs, improved my packaging, and answered every customer question quickly. Word spread, and more orders came in.

Of course, there were challenges. Sometimes materials were expensive, and my profits were small. Sometimes customers asked for changes that took hours to make. One time, a package got lost in the mail, and I had to send a new piece for free. Running a business was harder than I expected.

But there were also wonderful moments. A man wrote to say his wife cried when she opened her necklace. A mother sent me a photo of her daughter wearing my bracelet at her graduation. These messages reminded me why I started.

Now, three months later, I have left my office job. I work from home, making jewelry every day. The money is not as steady as before, and I work longer hours. But I feel alive. Every morning, I wake up excited to create something new.

My online shop is still small, but it is growing. I have learned that success does not happen overnight. It takes patience, hard work, and the courage to keep going when things are hard.

Starting this business was the scariest thing I have ever done. It was also the best decision of my life. If you have a dream you are afraid to try, I hope my story gives you courage. It is never too late to start something new.
