# A Simple Evening at Home

Tom is a student. He lives in a small apartment in the city with his sister, Anna. They are very busy people, but they like calm evenings at home. Today is Friday. Tom finishes school at 5:30, so he gets home **late**. Anna finishes work at 6:00, so she is at home before Tom.

When Tom opens the door, Anna is in the kitchen. She says, “You are **late** today!” Tom smiles and puts his bag on the chair. He says, “Yes, I know. The bus was slow, and there was a lot of traffic. But I am here **now**.”

Anna is happy to see him. She says, “Good, because it is time to start **dinner**.” Tom looks at the table. There are tomatoes, onions, rice, and chicken. Anna wants to **make** a simple meal. Tom likes cooking with his sister because they talk and laugh together.

Anna says, “Can you help me?” Tom answers, “Of course. What can I do **now**?” Anna gives him a big **box** from the kitchen shelf. The rice is **inside** the **box**. She says, “Please take the rice out of the **box** and wash it.” Tom does it carefully.

Then Anna says, “Please **carry** these vegetables to the table.” Tom takes the tomatoes and onions and starts to **carry** them. He almost drops one tomato, but Anna laughs and says, “Be careful!” Tom laughs too.

A little cat sits near the door. The cat’s name is Milo. Milo looks at the chicken and wants some food. Tom says, “Do not **let** Milo jump on the table!” Anna turns around quickly and says, “Milo, no!” She does not **let** the cat come near the food.

After that, Tom asks, “**Who** will cook the chicken today? You or me?” Anna says, “You can do it.” Tom is surprised. “Me? Are you sure?” Anna says, “Yes! I know you can **make** it well.”

Tom is a little nervous, but he starts to cook. Anna stands next to him and helps him. She tells him when to add salt and when to turn the chicken. Soon, the kitchen smells very good. Tom feels proud because he can **make** part of the meal by himself.

While they wait, Anna puts plates on the table. Tom brings water and bread. He helps **carry** everything from the kitchen to the dining table. Milo watches them from the floor. He is quiet **now**, but his eyes are still on the chicken.

When the food is ready, Anna says, “**Dinner** is ready!” Tom sits down and smiles. The rice from the **box** is soft and hot. The chicken is delicious. The salad is fresh. Tom says, “This is great. Thank you for your help.” Anna laughs and says, “You helped too!”

During **dinner**, they talk about their day. Tom says, “A new student came to class today.” Anna asks, “Oh really? **Who** is the new student?” Tom says, “Her name is Sara. She is very friendly.” Anna smiles and says, “Maybe you can talk to her again on Monday.”

After **dinner**, Tom helps clean the kitchen. He puts the rice **inside** the **box** again and closes it well. Anna washes the plates. Tom says, “I am tired **now**, but I am happy.” Anna says, “Me too. Even when you come home **late**, we can still have a nice evening.”

Before going to bed, Tom gives a little piece of chicken to Milo. Anna says, “Only a little. Do not **let** him eat too much!” Tom laughs. Then he asks, “**Who** will wake up early tomorrow to buy breakfast?” Anna points at Tom and smiles.

Tom says, “Not me! I want to sleep.” They laugh together. It is a simple evening, but it is warm and full of love.
