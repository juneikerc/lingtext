---
title: "A New Neighbor"
level: "a2"
date: "2026-05-08"
---

Two weeks ago, a new family moved into the house next door. I saw the moving truck early in the morning. Three men carried boxes, chairs, and a big bed into the house. A woman stood in the garden and told them where to put everything. She looked kind and friendly.

In the afternoon, I saw a girl about my age. She had long dark hair and was wearing a red jacket. She sat on the front steps and read a book. I wanted to say hello, but I felt a little shy. My mother noticed me looking and said, "Why don't you go and introduce yourself?"

I walked over and said, "Hi, my name is Lucy. I live next door." The girl looked up and smiled. "I'm Sara. We just moved here from another city. Everything feels new and strange."

We talked for a while. Sara told me that her father got a new job here and that her old school was much bigger than this one. She was worried about making new friends. I told her not to worry because our town is small but friendly. She also told me about her dog, Max. Max is a small brown dog who likes to run and play with balls. I told her that my family has a cat named Luna. Luna sleeps most of the day and only wakes up when she is hungry.

The next day, I took Sara to the park near our street. We walked there together, and I showed her the best places to play. There is a big tree where children like to sit and talk. There is also a small shop that sells ice cream for only one dollar. Sara was happy to see everything. She bought an ice cream, and I bought one too. We sat under the big tree and ate our ice cream while we watched some children play football.

After that, we went to my house. My mother made cookies and juice for us. Sara said my mother's cookies were the best she had ever eaten. We laughed and talked about our favorite movies and music. It turned out that we both liked the same singer.

Now Sara and I meet almost every day. We walk to school together in the morning and do our homework together in the evening. Last Saturday, we went to the cinema with my brother. The movie was funny, and we laughed a lot. My brother thinks Sara is very funny. He said he wants to go to the cinema with us again next month.

I am glad Sara moved here. She is a good friend, and I think she feels more comfortable now. When you move to a new place, it is important to have someone who can show you around. I am happy that I can be that person for her.
