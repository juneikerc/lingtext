# An Afternoon at the Library

Emma loved books. Every month, she tried to read at least two new books. But buying books was expensive, so she often went to the city library. It was free, quiet, and full of stories waiting to be discovered.

One Tuesday afternoon, Emma finished her classes early. She decided to spend a few hours at the library. She packed her bag with a notebook, a pen, and her water bottle.

The library was an old building in the center of town. It had tall windows and wooden floors. When Emma walked inside, she immediately felt calm. The smell of old paper and the soft sound of pages turning made her happy.

**Librarian:** Good afternoon. Do you need any help?

**Emma:** No, thank you. I am just looking for something new to read.

**Librarian:** We just got some new books in the fiction section. They are on the table near the window.

**Emma:** Great! I will look there first.

Emma walked to the fiction area. The new books looked interesting. There was a mystery novel, a love story, and a book about travel. She picked up the mystery book and read the first page.

**Emma:** This looks good. But I should look at more options.

She walked through the shelves. The library had thousands of books. Sometimes Emma thought that even if she read every day for her whole life, she could not read them all.

After twenty minutes, she had three books in her hands. She could only borrow two at a time, so she needed to choose.

She sat down at a table to read a little from each book. The first one was about a detective in London. The second was about a woman who moved to a small village. The third was a science fiction story about life on Mars.

Emma liked all three. This was going to be a difficult choice.

While she was reading, a girl about her age sat down at the same table. The girl had curly hair and glasses. She was carrying a large stack of books.

**Girl:** Is this seat free?

**Emma:** Yes, of course.

The girl sat down and opened a notebook. Emma noticed that she was studying. There were many notes and diagrams on her pages.

**Emma:** Are you preparing for an exam?

**Girl:** Yes. My math exam is on Friday. I am trying to understand these problems.

**Emma:** Math is not my favorite subject. Good luck!

**Girl:** Thank you. I need it!

They both smiled and continued working. Emma decided to borrow the mystery book and the book about the village. She would come back for the science fiction book another time.

She went to the front desk. The same librarian was there.

**Librarian:** Did you find something good?

**Emma:** Yes! These two, please.

The librarian scanned the books and Emma's library card.

**Librarian:** You can keep them for three weeks. If you need more time, you can renew them online.

**Emma:** Thank you. I will probably finish them soon.

**Librarian:** You are a fast reader. I remember you from last month.

**Emma:** Yes! I come here often.

With her books in her bag, Emma went back to her table. The girl was still studying. Emma decided to stay and read for a while.

She opened the mystery book. The story started with a missing painting in a museum. Emma was immediately interested. She read two chapters without stopping.

After an hour, she looked up. The library was getting more crowded. Students were coming in after school. Some were using computers. Others were looking for books like her.

Emma thought about how lucky they were to have this place. In a world of phones and fast information, the library was slow and peaceful. You could think here. You could imagine.

She looked at the girl next to her. The girl was now drawing something. Maybe a math problem was becoming clearer.

**Emma:** How is the studying going?

**Girl:** Better now. Sometimes I just need to draw things to understand them.

**Emma:** That is a good idea. I hope your exam goes well.

**Girl:** Thanks. Enjoy your books!

It was almost six o'clock. Emma packed her things and stood up. She waved goodbye to the girl and walked toward the exit.

At the door, she stopped and looked back. The library was beautiful in the evening light. The tall windows showed the orange sky outside. People were sitting at tables, lost in their books.

Emma smiled. She would be back soon.

Outside, the air was cool. She walked home slowly, thinking about the missing painting in her mystery book. She could not wait to find out what happened.

That night, she read until her eyes were tired. The library had given her another adventure, and it had not cost anything. That was the magic of books.
