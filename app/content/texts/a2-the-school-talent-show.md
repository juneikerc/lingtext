# The School Talent Show

Every year, Mill Creek School organized a talent show for its students. This year, Emma decided to participate. She was fourteen years old, and she had been playing the piano since she was six. Her best friend, Noah, also wanted to join. He planned to perform magic tricks.

They started practicing two weeks before the show. Emma practiced after school in the music room. Noah practiced in his backyard, hiding cards and colored scarves in his pockets.

**Noah:** Are you nervous?

**Emma:** A little. But I think it will be fun.

**Noah:** I am terrified. What if I drop the cards?

**Emma:** Then you pick them up. People like to see that you are human.

The night of the show arrived quickly. The school gymnasium was decorated with balloons and lights. Rows of chairs faced a small stage with a red curtain. Parents, teachers, and students filled the seats. The atmosphere was warm and exciting.

The first act was a group of third graders singing a popular song. They were cute but a little quiet. The audience clapped loudly anyway.

Then a boy from the sixth grade juggled three balls. He dropped one, but everyone laughed and cheered.

**Emma:** See? Mistakes are okay.

**Noah:** I hope you are right.

Emma was the seventh performer. She walked to the piano in a simple blue dress. The gym became very quiet. She took a deep breath and began to play. The music was soft at first, then stronger and faster. Her fingers moved across the keys like water. When she finished, the audience stood up and clapped for a long time.

**Emma:** Thank you. Thank you so much.

She walked back to her seat with a big smile.

**Noah:** That was incredible! You were amazing!

**Emma:** Now it is your turn.

Noah went on stage after two more acts. He wore a black hat and a purple cape. He looked funny but confident.

**Noah:** Good evening, everyone! My name is Noah, and I am going to show you some magic.

He made a coin disappear from his hand. The children in the front row gasped. Then he pulled a long ribbon from his empty sleeve. People laughed and clapped.

For his final trick, he asked a teacher to pick a card. The teacher showed it to everyone. It was the queen of hearts. Noah put the card back in the deck, shuffled the cards, and then threw the entire deck into the air. When the cards fell, only one remained in his hand.

**Noah:** Is this your card?

**Teacher:** Yes! How did you do that?

**Noah:** A magician never tells his secrets.

The audience laughed and applauded. Noah bowed and ran off the stage.

**Emma:** You were wonderful!

**Noah:** I almost dropped the cards twice.

**Emma:** But you did not. And you looked very natural.

At the end of the show, the principal came on stage to thank everyone. She did not announce a winner because, she said, every student who tried something new was already a winner.

**Principal:** Let us give a big round of applause to all our brave performers!

Emma and Noah walked home together. The night air was cool and fresh.

**Emma:** I am glad we did this.

**Noah:** Me too. Maybe next year we can do something together.

**Emma:** Like what?

**Noah:** You play the piano, and I do magic around you. A musical magic show!

**Emma:** That sounds like a great idea.

They talked about their plan all the way home. The talent show had been a little frightening, but it had also been one of the best nights of their year.
