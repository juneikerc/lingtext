---
title: "The Second-Hand Market"
level: "a2"
date: "2026-04-30"
---

Last Saturday, I went to a second-hand market with my cousin Anna. The market was in an old parking lot near the train station. People sold clothes, books, chairs, plates, toys, and many strange things. I did not need anything special, but Anna wanted to find a small desk for her room.

We arrived at nine in the morning. First, we walked slowly between the tables. Some sellers had everything in clean boxes. Other sellers had many things on blankets on the ground. The place was busy, but friendly. People talked, laughed, and asked about prices.

"Look at this jacket," Anna said.

The jacket was blue and looked almost new. Anna tried it on. It was a little big, but she liked it.

"How much is it?" she asked the seller.

"Ten euros," the woman said.

Anna smiled. "Can you make it eight?"

The woman thought for a moment. "Okay, eight."

Anna bought the jacket and put it in her bag. Then we continued walking.

At another table, I found a small radio. It was old, but it worked. The seller turned it on, and soft music came out. I liked the sound because it was warm and simple. I asked the price.

"Five euros," he said.

I did not buy it immediately. I walked away and thought about it. Did I really need a radio? No. But did I want it? Yes. After ten minutes, I went back and bought it.

Anna laughed. "You came here with no plan, and now you have a radio."

"That is the danger of markets," I said.

After that, we found the desk. It was small, white, and strong. It had two drawers. Anna checked the legs and opened the drawers. Everything was fine. The price was thirty euros, but she asked for twenty-five. The seller agreed because he did not want to take the desk home again.

The only problem was carrying it. The desk was not very heavy, but it was difficult to move through the crowd. We carried it together to the bus stop. Many people looked at us and smiled. One man said, "Nice desk!"

When we got to Anna's flat, we cleaned the desk with warm water and soap. Then we put it near the window. It looked perfect there. Anna put her computer on it and sat down.

"Now I can study better," she said.

I put my old radio on her shelf and turned it on. Music filled the room.

It was a simple day, but I enjoyed it. At a second-hand market, things have a past, and sometimes they get a new life. We saved money, found useful things, and had a good story to tell.
