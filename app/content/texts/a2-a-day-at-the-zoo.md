# A Day at the Zoo

Last Saturday, Maria and her little brother Carlos went to the zoo. They had been planning this trip for weeks. Maria was eighteen years old, and Carlos was ten. Their parents gave them money for tickets and lunch, and the two siblings took the bus early in the morning.

The zoo was outside the city. The bus ride took about forty minutes. Carlos sat by the window and looked at everything.

**Carlos:** Look, Maria! A big bridge!

**Maria:** Yes. We are getting close. The zoo is near the river.

When they arrived, many people were already there. Families with children, school groups, and couples were waiting in line at the entrance.

**Maria:** The line is long. But it is moving fast.

**Carlos:** I am so excited! I want to see the lions first.

**Maria:** Okay. We will see the lions. But first, we need to buy our tickets.

After fifteen minutes, they entered the zoo. Carlos ran to the map near the entrance.

**Carlos:** Where are the big animals?

**Maria:** Let me see. The lions are in Area C. We need to walk past the birds first.

They started walking. The first animals they saw were colorful parrots. The birds were very loud. Some of them could say "hello."

**Carlos:** That red parrot talked!

**Maria:** Yes. Some parrots learn words from people.

They took some photos of the birds. Then they continued to the next area. There were monkeys in big cages with trees and ropes.

**Carlos:** Look at that small monkey! He is eating a banana.

**Maria:** They look so funny when they eat.

One monkey came close to the glass. It looked at Carlos and made a funny face. Carlos laughed loudly.

**Carlos:** He likes me!

**Maria:** Or maybe he wants your sandwich.

They walked a little more and finally reached the lion area. It was very big, with grass and rocks. The lions looked strong and calm.

**Carlos:** Wow. They are huge!

**Maria:** The male lion is sleeping. But look, the female is walking.

They watched the lions for a long time. A zookeeper came and gave a talk about lion families. Carlos listened very carefully.

**Carlos:** Did you know lions sleep twenty hours a day?

**Maria:** Really? That is a lot of sleeping!

After the lions, they saw elephants. The elephants were eating grass and moving slowly. One baby elephant was playing with a ball.

**Carlos:** The baby is so cute!

**Maria:** Baby animals are always interesting to watch.

It was almost noon. Maria and Carlos started to feel hungry.

**Maria:** Let us find a place to eat. Do you want to sit down?

**Carlos:** Yes. My feet are tired.

They found a table near the giraffe area. While they ate their sandwiches, they watched the giraffes eat leaves from tall trees. The giraffes had very long necks.

**Carlos:** How do they sleep with such long necks?

**Maria:** Good question. I think they sleep standing up.

After lunch, they visited the reptile house. It was dark and warm inside. There were snakes, lizards, and even a big crocodile.

**Carlos:** The crocodile does not move at all.

**Maria:** He is waiting. Crocodiles can stay still for hours.

Carlos was a little scared of the snakes, so they did not stay long in that building.

Next, they went to see the penguins. The penguins were swimming in a cold pool. Some were standing on rocks, looking at the people.

**Maria:** They look like they are wearing little suits.

**Carlos:** They walk so funny!

The penguin area was very popular. Many children were watching and laughing.

In the afternoon, they visited the butterfly garden. It was a big room full of flowers and flying butterflies. The butterflies had many colors: blue, orange, yellow, and black.

**Carlos:** One landed on my hand!

**Maria:** Do not move. Let it stay there.

Carlos stood very still. The butterfly stayed on his hand for almost a minute, then flew away.

**Carlos:** That was amazing!

By four o'clock, they had seen almost everything. They were tired but very happy.

**Maria:** Do you want to buy a souvenir?

**Carlos:** Yes! I want a toy lion.

They went to the gift shop. Carlos chose a small stuffed lion. Maria bought a book about wild animals.

**Carlos:** Thank you for bringing me here, Maria.

**Maria:** I had a great time too. Maybe we can come back next year.

They took the bus home. Carlos fell asleep on Maria's shoulder. It had been a perfect day at the zoo.
