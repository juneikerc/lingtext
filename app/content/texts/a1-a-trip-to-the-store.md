---
title: "A trip to the store"
level: "a1"
---

Hello! My name is Anna. I live with my mother, my father, and my little brother. We have a small house in a nice town. We are a happy family, and we like to do things together.

Every morning, we have breakfast in the kitchen. We have bread, milk, eggs, and fruit. My father likes coffee, and my mother likes tea. My brother likes bananas very much. I like apples and bread with milk. We sit together and talk before the day starts.

One morning, my mother says, “We need food for dinner. Let’s go to the store.” I am happy because I like to go with her. I like to see many things in the store, and I like to help my mother buy food for the family.

Before we leave, my mother writes a list. On the list, we have rice, tomatoes, chicken, milk, bread, and apples. She asks me, “Do you know where the store is?” I say, “Yes, I know where it is. It is near the school.” My mother smiles and says, “Very well. Let’s go.”

We walk to the store together. The weather is good, and the sun is warm. In the street, we see many people. Some people go to work. Some children go to school. A woman has flowers. A man has a big bag. We walk slowly and talk about dinner.

Soon, we are at the store. We take a basket and go inside. The store is not very big, but it has all the things we need. First, we go to the fruit and vegetable area. My mother wants to buy tomatoes and apples. I help her choose good apples. They are red and fresh.

Then we go to the bread area. We buy two loaves of bread. After that, we go to the milk area. My mother says, “Please get two bottles of milk.” I take two bottles and put them in the basket. Then she says, “Can you get some yogurt too?” I say, “Yes!” and I get two small yogurts.

Next, we go to another part of the store. My mother wants chicken for dinner. We talk to a man who works in the store. He is very kind. My mother says, “We want to buy some chicken, please.” The man gives her the chicken. My mother says, “Thank you.” She is happy to get fresh chicken for the family.

Then we look at the list again. We still need rice. I see rice on a shelf and say, “Here it is!” My mother says, “Good! Please get one bag of rice.” I take one bag and put it in the basket. Now we have almost all the food on the list.

My mother asks, “Do we have cheese at home?” I say, “I do not know.” She says, “That is okay. We can buy some.” So we buy a little cheese too.

At the front of the store, we wait to pay. I see some chocolate near the door. My mother sees me look at it and smiles. She says, “You can get one chocolate bar today.” I am very happy. The woman at the cash register is nice. She puts all our food in bags.

Then we walk home. At home, my father and my brother are in the kitchen. My brother looks in the bag and says, “Apples!” He is very happy to get his favorite fruit. My father says, “Do we have all the food for dinner?” My mother says, “Yes, we do.”

In the evening, we have dinner together. We have chicken, rice, tomatoes, bread, and milk. The food is simple, but it is very good. I feel happy because I can help my family. I like to buy food, I like to go to the store, and I like to be with my family. I know where the store is, and I can help very well.
