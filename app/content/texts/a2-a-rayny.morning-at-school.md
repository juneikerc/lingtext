---
title: "A Rainy Morning at School"
level: "a2"
date: "2026-04-08"
---

Yesterday morning, it was cold and rainy. I woke up early, but I did not want to get out of bed. I could hear the rain on the window, and the sky was very dark. My room felt warm and quiet, so I wanted to stay there for a little longer. But I knew I had school, so I got up and looked outside. The street was wet, and people were walking fast with umbrellas.

I went to the kitchen and had a simple breakfast. I ate some toast and drank a cup of tea. My mother asked me if I wanted eggs too, but I said no because I was not very hungry. I was still sleepy, and the sound of the rain made me feel calm. After breakfast, I went back to my room, brushed my hair, packed my schoolbag, and checked my books for the day.

Then I got dressed and put on my jacket. I took my umbrella and walked to school. The air was cold, and there were many gray clouds in the sky. The streets were wet, and there were many cars. Some cars moved slowly because of the rain. I had to be careful when I crossed the road. On the way, I saw a woman with a red umbrella, two children in yellow raincoats, and a dog that was very wet but looked happy.

When I arrived at school, my shoes were a little dirty, but I was on time. Many students were in the hall because nobody wanted to stay outside. Some were talking loudly, some were laughing, and some were trying to dry their jackets. I met my friend Emma near the classroom door. She said, “I don’t like rainy mornings,” and I said, “Me neither, but at least we are here now.”

In class, our teacher asked us to write about our favorite season. I wrote about spring because I like flowers, green trees, and sunny days. I also like spring because the days are longer and the weather is usually nice. My friend Emma wrote about winter because she loves snow. She likes wearing warm clothes, drinking hot chocolate, and watching snow fall from the window. After writing, some students read their answers to the class. It was interesting because everyone had different ideas.

Later, we had a reading activity. The story was about a boy and his cat on a stormy day. I liked the story because it matched the weather outside. Then we did a few grammar exercises in our notebooks. The classroom was quiet for a while, and we could still hear the rain outside. Our teacher opened the window a little, and fresh air came in.

At break time, we stayed inside because of the rain. We talked, laughed, and shared some cookies. Emma had chocolate cookies, and I had apple pieces in a small box. We sat near the window and watched the rain fall in the playground. There were no students outside, and the swings moved a little in the wind. Even though the weather was not nice, the break felt fun because we were together.

In the afternoon, the rain became lighter. The sky was still gray, but it was not as dark as in the morning. Our last lesson was art, and that made me happy. We painted pictures of the weather. Some students painted storms, some painted sunny beaches, and I painted a park with wet trees and puddles on the ground.

When school finished, I walked home again with my umbrella. The streets were still wet, but the rain was softer now. I felt tired, but also relaxed. The day was gray outside, but inside the classroom, it felt warm and friendly. Sometimes rainy days are not exciting, but this one was calm, comfortable, and full of small good moments.
