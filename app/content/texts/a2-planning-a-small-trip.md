# Planning a Small Trip

Laura and her friend James want to go on a trip next weekend. They meet at a coffee shop on Thursday afternoon to plan everything. Laura arrives first and orders a hot chocolate. James comes ten minutes **late** because his bus was slow.

**Laura:** You are **late** again! What happened?

**James:** Sorry! The bus broke down, so I had to wait for the next one. But I am here **now**.

**Laura:** Okay, let us talk about our trip. Where do you want to go?

**James:** I was thinking about the mountains. We can rent a small cabin and stay there for two nights.

**Laura:** That sounds nice. But do we need to **book** it early?

**James:** Yes, we should **book** it today. Good cabins are **expensive**, and people take them quickly.

**Laura:** How **expensive** is it?

**James:** About eighty dollars per night. We can share the cost.

**Laura:** That is not too bad. Forty dollars each. I can **pay** that.

**James:** Great! I will **book** the cabin tonight. Do you want to see the photos first?

James shows Laura some photos on his phone. The cabin looks small but cozy. It has two bedrooms and a kitchen.

**Laura:** It looks perfect! But how will we get there?

**James:** We can take my car. It is old, but it works well.

**Laura:** Are you sure? Last time, your car stopped in the middle of the road.

**James:** That was different! I fixed the problem. The car is fine **now**.

**Laura:** Okay, I trust you. But if the car breaks again, you will **pay** for a taxi.

**James:** Fair enough. Now, what about food? Should we cook at the cabin?

**Laura:** Yes, cooking is cheaper than eating at restaurants. Plus, I like cooking!

**James:** What can you cook?

**Laura:** I can **make** pasta, soup, and pancakes for breakfast.

**James:** Pancakes! I love pancakes. Can you **make** them on Saturday morning?

**Laura:** Of course. But you need to help me **carry** the food from the car.

**James:** No problem. I am strong. I can **carry** many bags.

They laugh and drink their coffee. Then Laura remembers something important.

**Laura:** Oh! What about the weather? We need to check if it will rain.

**James:** Good point. Let me look at the weather app.

James checks his phone. The weather forecast says sunny on Saturday and cloudy on Sunday.

**James:** Saturday will be sunny, but Sunday might have some clouds.

**Laura:** That is okay. We can hike on Saturday and relax at the cabin on Sunday.

**James:** Perfect plan. Should we invite anyone else?

**Laura:** I think just the two of us is better. We can talk more and enjoy the quiet.

**James:** You are right. Sometimes small groups are better than big ones.

**Laura:** Exactly. Now, what time should we leave on Friday?

**James:** If we leave at four o'clock, we can arrive before dark.

**Laura:** Four o'clock works for me. I will finish work at three.

**James:** I will pick you up at your apartment. Do not be **late**!

**Laura:** Me? **Late**? Never!

They both laugh because Laura is usually the one who arrives late.

**James:** One more thing. Should we bring games or books?

**Laura:** Let us bring a board game. Something fun for two players.

**James:** I have a game called "Word Journey." We can practice English while we play.

**Laura:** That is a great idea! I want to improve my English.

**James:** Your English is already good. But practice always helps.

**Laura:** Thank you. You are a good friend.

**James:** You too. I am excited about this trip.

**Laura:** Me too. It will be a nice break from work.

**James:** Okay, so I will **book** the cabin tonight and **pay** online. Then I will send you the address.

**Laura:** Perfect. And I will **make** a list of food to buy.

**James:** We can go shopping together on Friday morning.

**Laura:** Good idea. Then we can **carry** everything to the car together.

**James:** This trip will be amazing. I can feel it.

**Laura:** I think so too. Two days in the mountains, away from the city.

**James:** Fresh air, quiet nights, and your famous pancakes.

**Laura:** Do not forget the hiking!

**James:** Yes, the hiking too. We will take many photos.

**Laura:** I will bring my camera.

**James:** And I will bring my guitar. We can sing songs at night.

**Laura:** That sounds magical. I cannot wait!

They finish their coffee and stand up to leave. Laura checks her bag.

**Laura:** Do you have everything? Phone, wallet, keys?

**James:** Yes, everything is here. I am ready to go.

**Laura:** See you on Friday at four. Do not be **late**!

**James:** I promise I will be on time. This trip is important to me.

**Laura:** To me too. See you soon!

**James:** See you soon! And get ready for an adventure!

They wave goodbye and go their separate ways. Both of them are smiling. They know the weekend will be special.
