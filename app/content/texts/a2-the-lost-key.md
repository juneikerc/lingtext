# Chapter 1 — A Bad Morning

Ana **woke up** late on Saturday morning. She **looked at** her phone and **saw** it was already ten o'clock. "Oh no!" she **said**. "I **need** to **meet** my friend at the café in thirty minutes!"

She **jumped** out of **bed** and **got dressed** quickly. She **didn't have** time for **breakfast**, so she **put** an apple in her **bag** and **ran** out of her **apartment**.

Ana **walked** fast through the **streets** of the small **town**. The sun was **shining** and the **weather** was **warm**. She **felt** happy because she **loved** weekends with her friends.

She **arrived** at the café at ten thirty. Her friend Maria was already there, **sitting** at a **table** near the **window**.

"**Sorry** I'm **late**!" Ana **said** as she **sat down**.

"No **problem**," Maria **smiled**. "I just **ordered** a **coffee** for you."

They **talked** and **laughed** for two hours. They **discussed** their **plans** for the **weekend**. Maria **wanted** to go to the **beach**, but Ana **preferred** to **visit** the old **castle** outside of town.

At twelve thirty, they **decided** to **leave**. Ana **reached** into her **bag** for her **wallet**, but her **hand** **touched** something **strange**. Her **keys** weren't there.

"**Wait**," she **said**, **feeling** **worried**. "I **can't find** my **keys**!"

She **looked** in her bag **again**. She **checked** her **pockets**. Nothing. The **keys** were **gone**.

"Maria, I **think** I **lost** my apartment **keys**!" Ana **said**, her **face** turning **white**. "What am I going to **do**? My **roommate** is **away** for the weekend!"

# Chapter 2 — The Search Begins

"**Don't worry**," Maria **said** **calmly**. "Let's **think** about this. **Where** did you **last** **see** your keys?"

Ana **closed** her **eyes** and **tried** to **remember**. "This **morning**," she **began**, "I **woke up** late and **left** my apartment in a **hurry**. I **remember** **locking** the **door** with my keys. Then I **put** them in my **bag**."

"**Maybe** they **fell** out in the café?" Maria **suggested**.

They **asked** the **waiter** if anyone had **found** a set of keys. The waiter **shook** his **head**. "**Sorry**, no one has **brought** any keys here **today**."

Ana **felt** **disappointed**. "I **need** to **check** my bag **again**," she **said**. She **emptied** **everything** onto the table: her **wallet**, her **phone**, her **book**, some **receipts**, and the **apple** she **didn't eat**.

"No keys," she **said** **sadly**.

"Let's **retrace** your **steps**," Maria **suggested**. "**Where** did you **walk** this **morning**?"

"I **came** **straight** from my apartment to here," Ana **explained**. "I **didn't** **stop** anywhere. I just **walked** along Main **Street** and then **turned** onto Oak **Avenue**."

They **decided** to **walk** the same **route** **backwards**. They **left** the café and **started** **walking** slowly. Ana **looked** at the **ground** carefully, **hoping** to **see** something **shiny**.

"Do you **think** someone **stole** them?" Ana **asked** **nervously**.

"I **don't think** so," Maria **replied**. "Keys aren't **valuable** to **strangers**. They probably just **fell** out of your bag."

They **walked** past a **bakery**, a **clothing** **store**, and a small **park**. Ana **asked** a **woman** who was **walking** her **dog** if she had seen any keys.

"No, **dear**," the woman **said** **kindly**. "But I'll **keep** my **eyes** **open** for you."

When they **reached** the **corner** of Main Street and Oak Avenue, Ana **stopped**. "**Wait**," she **said**. "I **remember** something! I **stopped** here this **morning** to **tie** my **shoe**. Maybe my keys **fell** out then?"

# Chapter 3 — Retracing Steps

Ana and Maria **looked** carefully around the **corner** where Main Street **meets** Oak Avenue. There was a **bench**, a **trash** **can**, and some **bushes**.

"Let me **check** under the **bench**," Maria **offered**. She **bent down** and **looked** under the **wooden** **seat**. "**Nothing** here **except** some old **gum** and a **lost** **glove**."

Ana **searched** through the **bushes** with her **hands**, **hoping** to **feel** the **cold** **metal** of her keys. "They're not here **either**," she **said** **frustrated**.

An **old man** **sitting** on a **nearby** **bench** **watched** them with **curiosity**. "**Excuse** me," he **called** out. "Are you **looking** for something **important**?"

"**Yes**," Ana **answered**. "I **lost** my apartment keys this **morning**."

The old man **thought** for a **moment**. "I **saw** something **shiny** on the **ground** **earlier**," he **said**. "It was near the **flower** **shop** down the **street**."

"**Really**?" Ana **asked** **excitedly**. "**Thank** you!"

They **thanked** the man and **walked** quickly toward the flower shop. It was a small **store** with **beautiful** **plants** in the **window**. Ana **went** inside and **spoke** to the **owner**.

"**Excuse** me, did anyone **bring** you some keys **today**?" she **asked**.

The owner, a **friendly** woman with **gray** **hair**, **shook** her head. "I'm **sorry**, no keys here. But I **hope** you **find** them **soon**!"

Ana **sighed**. "This is **impossible**," she **said** to Maria. "I've **looked** everywhere!"

"**Don't** **give up** yet," Maria **encouraged** her. "Let's **think** about this **differently**. What **else** did you **do** this morning before the café?"

"**Well**..." Ana **paused**. "Oh! I **remember** now! I **stopped** at the **mailbox** on **Green** **Street** to **send** a **letter** to my **mother**!"

"**Perfect**!" Maria **said**. "Let's go there **next**."

They **hurried** to Green Street. The **mailbox** was **red** and **standing** on the **corner**. Ana **looked** all around it, under it, and even **inside** the **mail** **slot**.

"Still **nothing**," Ana **said** **quietly**. She **sat** down on the **curb** and **put** her **head** in her **hands**. "What am I going to **do**? I can't **enter** my apartment. I can't **call** my **landlord** until **Monday**."

# Chapter 4 — A Helpful Stranger

As Ana **sat** on the **curb** **feeling** **sad**, a **young** man **approached** them. He was **wearing** a **blue** **uniform** and **carrying** a **bag**.

"**Excuse** me," he **said** **politely**. "Are you **okay**? You **look** **upset**."

"I **lost** my apartment keys," Ana **explained**. "I've **looked** everywhere, but I can't **find** them."

"I'm **Tom**," the man **introduced** himself. "I **work** at the **post** **office** on Green Street. I **saw** you this **morning** when you **mailed** your **letter**."

"**Really**?" Ana **asked** **surprised**.

"**Yes**," Tom **nodded**. "I **remember** because you were in a **hurry**. You **almost** **forgot** to put a **stamp** on your **envelope**."

Ana **laughed** a little. "That **sounds** like me! I'm always **forgetting** things when I'm **late**."

"**Well**," Tom **continued**, "after you **left**, I **noticed** something **shiny** on the **ground** near the **mailbox**. I **thought** about **picking** it up, but then a **delivery** **truck** **came** and I had to **move** my **bike**. When I **came** **back**, the **shiny** thing was **gone**."

"**Someone** **took** it?" Maria **asked**.

"No, I **think** it just **got** **kicked** **under** the **mailbox** or into the **gutter**," Tom **said**. "The **street** **cleaner** **came** through about an **hour** **ago**."

Ana's **eyes** **widened**. "The **street** **cleaner**? Do you **think** he **found** my keys?"

"**Maybe**," Tom **shrugged**. "I can **help** you **find** him if you **want**. I **know** his **route**."

"**Would** you **really** **help** us?" Ana **asked** **gratefully**.

"Of **course**!" Tom **smiled**. "**Follow** me."

They **walked** with Tom through the **streets** of the town. He **knew** everyone and **greeted** many **shop** **owners** along the **way**. Finally, they **found** the **street** **cleaner** **emptying** a **trash** **can** near the **town** **square**.

"**Hey**, **George**!" Tom **called**. "Did you **find** any keys **today**?"

George, a **tall** man with a **white** **beard**, **turned** around. "**Keys**?" he **repeated**. "**Actually**, **yes**! I **found** some **silver** keys near the **mailbox** on Green Street this **morning**. I **put** them in my **pocket** **planning** to **take** them to the **police** **station** later."

Ana **gasped**. "Those are **mine**!" she **exclaimed**. "Can I **please** have them **back**?"

# Chapter 5 — The Happy Ending

George **looked** at Ana **carefully**. "How do I **know** these are your **keys**?" he **asked**.

"**Well**," Ana **said** quickly, "there are **three** keys on the **ring**. One **big** key for the **apartment** **door**, one **small** key for my **bedroom**, and a **brass** key for the **mailbox**. Oh, and there's also a **red** **keychain** with a **little** **cat** on it."

George **smiled** and **reached** into his **pocket**. He **pulled** out a set of keys with a **red** cat **keychain**. "**Exactly** **right**," he **said**, **handing** them to Ana. "Here you **go**, **young** **lady**."

"**Thank** you so **much**!" Ana **said**, her **eyes** **filling** with **tears** of **happiness**. She **hugged** George **unexpectedly**.

"**Whoa** there!" George **laughed** **surprised**. "You're **welcome**! Just **be** more **careful** **next** **time**."

"I **will**, I **promise**!" Ana **said**.

Tom was **smiling** too. "I'm **glad** we **found** them," he **said**. "Now you can **relax** and **enjoy** your **weekend**."

"**Thanks** to you **too**, Tom," Ana **said**. "You were so **kind** to **help** us."

"No **problem** at all," Tom **replied**. "That's what **neighbors** are for."

Maria **hugged** Ana. "I told you **not** to **worry**!" she **laughed**. "Now, **how** about that **coffee** you **owe** me? This **search** made me **hungry**!"

They all **laughed** together. Ana **felt** **relieved** and **happy**. She **learned** an **important** **lesson** that **day**.

That **evening**, Ana **sat** in her **apartment** with a **cup** of **tea**. She **looked** at her keys on the **table**. She had **attached** them to a **long** **chain** that **hooked** to her **bag**.

"**Never** **again**," she **said** to herself. "From now on, I will **always** **check** my **bag** before I **leave** anywhere. And I will **never** **rush** in the **morning** **again**!"

She **picked** up her **phone** and **sent** a **message** to her **roommate**: "**Guess** what **happened** **today**? I **lost** our keys but **found** them **again**. **Long** **story**! I'll **tell** you when you **get** **home**."

Then she **smiled**, **turned** off the **light**, and **went** to **sleep** - with her keys **safely** under her **pillow**, just for **tonight**.

**The** **End**
