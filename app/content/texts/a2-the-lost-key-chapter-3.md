# The Lost Key - Chapter 3: Retracing Steps

Ana and Maria **looked** carefully around the **corner** where Main Street **meets** Oak Avenue. There was a **bench**, a **trash** **can**, and some **bushes**.

"Let me **check** under the **bench**," Maria **offered**. She **bent down** and **looked** under the **wooden** **seat**. "**Nothing** here **except** some old **gum** and a **lost** **glove**."

Ana **searched** through the **bushes** with her **hands**, **hoping** to **feel** the **cold** **metal** of her keys. "They're not here **either**," she **said** **frustrated**.

An **old man** **sitting** on a **nearby** **bench** **watched** them with **curiosity**. "**Excuse** me," he **called** out. "Are you **looking** for something **important**?"

"**Yes**," Ana **answered**. "I **lost** my apartment keys this **morning**."

The old man **thought** for a **moment**. "I **saw** something **shiny** on the **ground** **earlier**," he **said**. "It was near the **flower** **shop** down the **street**."

"**Really**?" Ana **asked** **excitedly**. "**Thank** you!"

They **thanked** the man and **walked** quickly toward the flower shop. It was a small **store** with **beautiful** **plants** in the **window**. Ana **went** inside and **spoke** to the **owner**.

"**Excuse** me, did anyone **bring** you some keys **today**?" she **asked**.

The owner, a **friendly** woman with **gray** **hair**, **shook** her head. "I'm **sorry**, no keys here. But I **hope** you **find** them **soon**!"

Ana **sighed**. "This is **impossible**," she **said** to Maria. "I've **looked** everywhere!"

"**Don't** **give up** yet," Maria **encouraged** her. "Let's **think** about this **differently**. What **else** did you **do** this morning before the café?"

"**Well**..." Ana **paused**. "Oh! I **remember** now! I **stopped** at the **mailbox** on **Green** **Street** to **send** a **letter** to my **mother**!"

"**Perfect**!" Maria **said**. "Let's go there **next**."

They **hurried** to Green Street. The **mailbox** was **red** and **standing** on the **corner**. Ana **looked** all around it, under it, and even **inside** the **mail** **slot**.

"Still **nothing**," Ana **said** **quietly**. She **sat** down on the **curb** and **put** her **head** in her **hands**. "What am I going to **do**? I can't **enter** my apartment. I can't **call** my **landlord** until **Monday**."
