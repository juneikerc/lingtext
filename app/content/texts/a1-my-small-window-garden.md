---
title: "My Small Window Garden"
level: "a1"
date: "2026-04-30"
---

I live in a small flat. I do not have a big garden. But I have a window. The window is in my kitchen. It gets sun in the morning.

On Monday, I buy three small plants. One plant is green mint. One plant is red tomato. One plant is yellow flower. I carry them home in a bag.

My mother looks at the plants and smiles. "They are nice," she says. "Do you know how to care for them?"

"I can learn," I say.

I put the plants near the window. I give them water. I do not give too much water. The soil is wet, but not very wet.

Every morning, I look at my plants. I say, "Good morning." It is funny, but I like it. The plants are quiet friends.

On Tuesday, the mint smells good. I take one small leaf. I put it in my tea. The tea tastes fresh.

On Wednesday, the tomato plant has a new leaf. It is very small. I am happy. I call my sister.

"My tomato plant has a new leaf," I say.

"That is good," she says. "You are a plant teacher now."

"No," I say. "I am a plant student."

On Thursday, the yellow flower looks sad. Its head is down. I think it needs more sun. I move it closer to the window. I give it a little water.

On Friday, the flower looks better. Its head is up. I feel proud.

On Saturday, my friend Leo visits me. He sees the plants.

"Your kitchen looks happy," he says.

"Yes," I say. "The plants make the room nice."

We make lunch. We have bread, cheese, and soup. I put some mint in the soup. Leo likes it.

"Can I have a small plant too?" he asks.

"Yes," I say. "We can go to the store tomorrow."

Now I love my window garden. It is small, but it is important to me. I give the plants water. They give me color, smell, and a happy morning.
