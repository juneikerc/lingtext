---
title: "My First Day at Work"
level: "a2"
date: "2026-04-22"
---

Last month, I got a new job at a coffee shop. I was nervous and excited. It was my first real job, and I wanted to do everything right.

My manager is a kind woman named Mrs. Brown. She showed me around the shop on my first day. There were many things to learn.

"First, I will teach you how to make coffee," she said. "It looks hard, but you will learn fast."

I watched her carefully. She put coffee in the machine and pressed a button. Hot coffee came out into a cup. It looked easy, but when I tried, I made a mess.

"Do not worry," Mrs. Brown said. "Everyone makes mistakes on the first day. Try again."

I tried three more times. After that, my coffee looked better. Mrs. Brown smiled. "Good job! Now you can practice more."

Next, she taught me how to use the cash register. Some customers paid with cash, others used cards. I had to count change and give receipts. This was harder than making coffee.

A customer came in. She was an older woman with a friendly face.

"Good morning," I said. "What can I get for you?"

"A small coffee, please," she said. "And one of those muffins."

I wrote down her order. My hands were shaking a little. I did not want to make a mistake.

The coffee machine made its loud noise. I poured the coffee into a cup and put it on a tray with the muffin. Then I told her the price.

"That will be four dollars and fifty cents, please."

She gave me a five-dollar bill. I counted her change: fifty cents. I gave her the receipt too.

"Thank you very much," I said.

"You are very welcome," she said. "It is nice to see a new face here. Good luck with your first day!"

Her kind words made me feel better. More customers came in, and each time I felt less nervous.

At lunch time, Mrs. Brown told me to take a break. I sat in the back room and ate my sandwich. Another worker, a young man named Carlos, sat with me.

"How is your first day?" he asked.

"It is good," I said. "I was afraid at first, but now I feel more comfortable."

"I was the same," Carlos said. "After one week, everything will feel normal. You will see."

His words gave me hope. After lunch, I worked for four more hours. I made many coffees and talked to many customers. Some were friendly, others were in a hurry. I learned to work quickly.

At the end of the day, Mrs. Brown asked me to sit down.

"You did very well today," she said. "You learn fast, and you are polite with customers. I think you will be a great worker here."

"Thank you so much," I said. "I really want to do a good job."

"You already are," she said. "See you tomorrow at eight."

I walked home feeling proud. The day was tiring, but I learned so much. I made some mistakes, but I also did many things right. My first day at work was a success, and I could not wait to go back.
