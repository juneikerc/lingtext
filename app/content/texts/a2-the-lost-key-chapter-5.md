# The Lost Key - Chapter 5: The Happy Ending

George **looked** at Ana **carefully**. "How do I **know** these are your **keys**?" he **asked**.

"**Well**," Ana **said** quickly, "there are **three** keys on the **ring**. One **big** key for the **apartment** **door**, one **small** key for my **bedroom**, and a **brass** key for the **mailbox**. Oh, and there's also a **red** **keychain** with a **little** **cat** on it."

George **smiled** and **reached** into his **pocket**. He **pulled** out a set of keys with a **red** cat **keychain**. "**Exactly** **right**," he **said**, **handing** them to Ana. "Here you **go**, **young** **lady**."

"**Thank** you so **much**!" Ana **said**, her **eyes** **filling** with **tears** of **happiness**. She **hugged** George **unexpectedly**.

"**Whoa** there!" George **laughed** **surprised**. "You're **welcome**! Just **be** more **careful** **next** **time**."

"I **will**, I **promise**!" Ana **said**.

Tom was **smiling** too. "I'm **glad** we **found** them," he **said**. "Now you can **relax** and **enjoy** your **weekend**."

"**Thanks** to you **too**, Tom," Ana **said**. "You were so **kind** to **help** us."

"No **problem** at all," Tom **replied**. "That's what **neighbors** are for."

Maria **hugged** Ana. "I told you **not** to **worry**!" she **laughed**. "Now, **how** about that **coffee** you **owe** me? This **search** made me **hungry**!"

They all **laughed** together. Ana **felt** **relieved** and **happy**. She **learned** an **important** **lesson** that **day**.

That **evening**, Ana **sat** in her **apartment** with a **cup** of **tea**. She **looked** at her keys on the **table**. She had **attached** them to a **long** **chain** that **hooked** to her **bag**.

"**Never** **again**," she **said** to herself. "From now on, I will **always** **check** my **bag** before I **leave** anywhere. And I will **never** **rush** in the **morning** **again**!"

She **picked** up her **phone** and **sent** a **message** to her **roommate**: "**Guess** what **happened** **today**? I **lost** our keys but **found** them **again**. **Long** **story**! I'll **tell** you when you **get** **home**."

Then she **smiled**, **turned** off the **light**, and **went** to **sleep** - with her keys **safely** under her **pillow**, just for **tonight**.

**The** **End**
